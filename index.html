questions we've asked ourselves

why do we miss places more than people sometimes?

spaces are stable. people change. in a cafe corner, we can encounter past versions of ourselves without the weight of all the ways things have shifted. that permanence is comforting.

is it okay to feel nostalgic for something that's still happening?

absolutely. we can grieve the ending of moments while they're still unfolding. that bittersweet awarenessâ€”that nothing lastsâ€”is what makes presence real. it's not sadness. it's love.

how do we hold memories without holding them too tight?

let them live in soft focus. write them down, then release the need to perfectly preserve every detail. the feeling is what matters. the essence stays, and that's enough.

what's the point of writing it down if we already remember?

the act of writing transforms remembering. it makes the intangible real. it's witness to your own life. someday, reading your words will let you time-travel in the most gentle way.

can a digital space feel like home?

home is where your truths are held safely. if this space feels like a warm corner where you can be honest about what you miss and cherish, then yes. it can be home.

what do you do with all this longing?

you transform it into presence. you take what you learned in those places and people, and you build something new with it. you become the cafe for someone else.
